# Style Notes

## Tone
- Professional and direct
- No marketing language
- Assumes the user knows fintech/benefits domain

---

## Capitalization rules (official, from design system)

### Book Style Capitalization
Capitalize all words **except** prepositions shorter than 5 letters (with, from, at, etc.), conjunctions (and, or, but), and articles (a, an, the). Always capitalize the first and last word regardless.

**Use for:**
- Titles (list view columns, dialogs, and so on)
- Buttons (functions)

### Sentence Style Capitalization
Capitalize only the first letter of the first word and proper names.

**Use for:**
- Labels
- Tooltips
- Descriptive text
- Selectable items (drop-downs)
- Pop-up titles

## Copy patterns by UI element type

### Page & modal titles
- **Sentence case** (pop-up titles use sentence style per design system)
- Descriptive and specific — avoid generic labels like "Settings" or "Details"

### Field labels
- **Sentence case**: "Excess amount", "Refund method", "Spend limits"
- Short — 1–3 words preferred

### Field support text (below a label)
- Sentence case, no period unless 2 sentences
- Starts with a noun, not a verb: "Amount entered will be debited from your HSA."
- Exception: verb-first when the field is part of a list/card description

### Tooltips
- Full sentences, sentence case, period at the end
- Neutral and factual — no "easily", no "simply"
- Exception: "Effortlessly upload files..." is an outlier — avoid this pattern
- Pattern: "Limits the [what] that can be [action] [scope/condition]."

### Error messages & warnings
- Sentence case, period at the end
- Action-oriented: tell the user what to do, not just what went wrong
- "Please convert the image into an approved file type or ask your provider for an updated receipt as a PDF."

### Warning / consent blocks
- Formal tone — these are legal or compliance statements
- Full sentences with periods
- Use precise legal terminology: "tax filing due date", "Federal Income Tax Return"
- Checkbox confirmation: "I understand the above rules" — short, no period

### Contextual links & inline prompts
- Sentence case
- Frame as a question or conditional: "Have a bill you haven't paid yet?"
- Link text is action-based: "Pay a Bill"

### Notification / announcement copy
- Conversational, warmer tone allowed: "We're Listening!"
- Keep body under 2 sentences

## Domain terminology (use without explanation)
- HSA, HRA, FSA, HCFSA, COBRA
- Notional accounts
- Forfeitures
- Reimbursements
- Contributions / Payroll contributions
- Plan year / Spend period
- Enrollment / Deductible
- EOB (Explanation of Benefits)
- IRS limit / IRS annual limit
- Excess contributions / Excess earnings
